Features & Traits

Background: Guild of Gastronomy

  • Skill Proficiency Investigation, Insight
  • Feature You have the Chef Feature.
  • Equipment A brass broach of a fork over a knife, and Cook's Utensils.

Feature: Chef's Hospitality. You will help and are helped by those in your guild, and most chefs will offer food and shelter in return for service.

Feature: Chef

Time and effort spent mastering the culinary arts has paid off. You gain the following benefits:

  • Increase your Constitution or Wisdom by 1, to a maximum of 20.
  • You gain proficiency with cook's utensils if you don't already have it.
  • As part of a short rest, you can cook special food, provided you have ingredients and cook's utensils on hand. You can prepare enough of this food for a number of creatures equal to 4 + your proficiency bonus. At the end of the short rest, any creature who eats the food and spends one or more Hit Dice to regain hit points regains an extra 1d8 hit points.
  • With one hour of work or when you finish a long rest, you can cook a number of treats equal to your proficiency bonus. These special treats last 8 hours after being made. A creature can use a bonus action to eat one of those treats to gain temporary hit points equal to your proficiency bonus.

Beastform: Bear

As an action, you can transform into a Bear, or back into your true form (which is humanoid). Your statistics change as outlined below, if not listed, they remain the same. Any equipment worn or carried isn't transformed. You revert to your true form when unconscious or dead.

When in Beastform, you advantage on ability checks that rely on smell and sound.

As a Bear, you have the following attributes:

  • Size becomes Large.
  • Speed becomes 40ft. and gain a 30ft. climbing speed.
  • AC becomes 10 + Strength Modifier
  • HP Max and HP are doubled (and are halved when reverting to your true form)

As a Bear, you have the following attacks:

  • Bite. Melee Weapon Attack: +6 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: (3d4 + 4) piercing damage.
  • Claw. Melee Weapon Attack: +6 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: (3d4 + 4) slashing damage.

Stress

You have 4 Stress Points; Stress points are accumulated when failing a DC (10 + 2 for every stress point) Wisdom Save. These saves are required when damaged or aggravated. When all stress Point are accumulated you must spend Stress Points as quickly as possible. You unlock new ways to spend Stress Points at later levels. A short rest reduces Stress by half while a long rest reduces Stress completely.

Level 1:

  • Wild Shift. If you are not in Beastform: Bear, you transform into Beastform: Bear.
  • Savage Strike. As an action, you attack the closest creature, friend or foe.

Level 3:

  • Frenzy. You enter a frenzy, attacking with advantage and are similarly attacked at advantage.
  • Lash. As a bonus action, you attack the closest creature, friend or foe.

Level 5:

  • Bleed. Your AC becomes 10 for the next round, but your next attack bleeds the enemy for an additional Martial Die at the beginning of their next turn.

Unarmoured Defence

While you wear no armour and are not wielding a shield, your AC equals 10 + your Dexterity modifier + your Wisdom modifier.

Martial Arts

Your practice of martial arts gives you mastery of unarmed strikes. Gaining you the following benefits:

  • Your unarmed strikes deal 1d4 + Strength Mod Bludgeoning Damage. This die changes as you gain monk levels, as shown in the Martial Arts column of the Monk table.
  • When you use the Attack action with an unarmed strike on your turn, you can make one unarmed strike as a bonus action, assuming you haven't already taken a bonus action this turn.

Ki

Starting at 2nd level, your training allows you to harness the mystic energy of ki. Your access to this energy is represented by a number of ki points. Your monk level determines the number of points you have, as shown in the Ki Points column of the Monk table.

You can spend these points to fuel various ki features. You learn the following skills, and others as you gain levels in this class.

Flurry of Blows. Immediately after you take the Attack action on your turn, you can spend 1 ki point to make two unarmed strikes as a bonus action.

Patient Defence. You can spend 1 ki point to take the Dodge action as a bonus action on your turn.

Step of the Wind. You can spend 1 ki point to take the Disengage or Dash action as a bonus action on your turn, and your jump distance is doubled for the turn.

When you spend a ki point, it is unavailable until you finish a short or long rest, at the end of which you draw all of your expended ki back into yourself. You must spend at least 30 minutes of the rest meditating to regain your ki points.

Some of your ki features require your target to make a saving throw to resist the feature's effects. The saving throw DC is calculated as follows:

Ki save DC = 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Wisdom modifier

Way of the Forest

Monks of the Way of the Forest follow their animal instincts to grant stealth and strength. These monks are often seen collaborating and sharing knowledge with Druids. Their skills are widley unknown and are likely used to aid in hunting and gathering.

You gain access to the following Druid Spells at the described level:

Mold Earth. At 2nd level you learn this cantrip and can cast it at will.

Purify Food and Drink. At 2nd level, you can spend 1 ki point to cast this spell, or cast it ritually for no cost.

Pass Without a Trace. At 3rd level, you can spend 2 ki points to cast this spell.

Meld into Stone. At 5th level, you can spend 4 ki points to cast this spell.

Guardian of Nature. At 7th level, you can spend 6 ki points to cast this spell.

Awaken. At 9th level, you can spend 9 ki points and the required material components, to cast this spell.
