Brujo

Hit Points

Hit Dice: 1d6

Hit Points at 1st Level: 6 + your Constitution modifier

Hit Points at Higher Levels: 1d6 (or 4) + your Constitution modifier per Brujo level after 1st

Proficiencies

Armour: none

Weapons: daggers, shortswords, darts, slings, quarterstaffs

Tools: herbalism kit

Saving Throws: Constitution, Charisma

Skills: Choose 2 from Arcana, Deception, Insight, Intimidation, Persuasion, and Religion.

Starting Equipment

You start with the following items, plus anything provided by your background:

  • A dagger and a herbalism kit
  • (a) A shortsword or (b) A quarterstaff
  • (a) A dungeoneer's pack or (b) An explorer's pack

Unworldly Tinkerer - lvl. 1

You have learnt how to transmute flora and fauna into unworldly creations. This is your way of harnessing the magic that flows in your blood. To observers, you don't appear to be casting spells, but rather utilising alien creatures that manifest magical feats. These Constructs are parasitic to your arcane energy and will wither and die when away from you for an extended period of time.

At 1st level, you gain proficiency with Herbalism Kit, which is required in the creation of Constructs. If you already have proficiency, you instead have expertise with Herbalism Kit.

Cantrips

At 1st level, you have perfected four unworldly Constructs, represented as Cantrips and are not destroyed upon use. You can select these from the Sorcerer and Druid Cantrips Lists.

Preparing and Creating Constructs

The Constructs Table shows how many Constructs you can have prepared at any given time and at any given level. Upon using one of these Constructs, it is expended and destroyed.

It requires 30 minutes and 5 gold per level of the spell, to make one of these Constructs. The gold represents the price of procuring the necessary ingredients, these can otherwise be appropriately harvested.

The Brujo has an innate understanding of how to make these constructs knowing Recipes (Spells) equal to the number of prepared Constructs Table plus 2 from the Sorcerer and Druid Spell Lists. These cannot be unlearned or replaced at a later date.

Spellcasting Ability

Charisma is your spellcasting ability for your Constructs, since the power of your magic relies on your ability to project your will into these creatures. You use your Charisma whenever a spell refers to your spellcasting ability. In addition, you use your Charisma modifier when setting the saving throw DC for a sorcerer spell you cast and when making an attack roll with one.

Spell Save DC = 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Charisma modifier
Spell Attack Modifier = Your proficiency bonus + your Charisma modifier

Unworldly Origin - lvl. 1

You have been touched by an alien entity and bestowed a fragment of their power. You have been fundamentally changed by this, which awakens powerful magical abilities inside you, often altering your appearance in some way.

At 1st level, you choose one of the cleric class's divine domains. You add that domain spells for 1st-level clerics to your known spells. These spells do not count against the number of spells you can know, and they are considered to be sorcerer spells for you. When you reach 3rd, 5th, 7th, and 9th levels in the sorcerer class, you likewise learn your domain's spells that become available at those levels.

Bonus Proficiency

At 1st level, you are proficient in an additional Brujo skill available at character creation.

Transmogrification Initiate - lvl. 2

At 2nd level, you tap into the creative forces that reside within yours. This force is represented by your Transmog Points, which allow you to modify your magical effects.

Transmog Points

You have 2 Transmog Points initially, gaining one additional point every time you level up, to a maximum of 20 at level 20. You can never have more Transmog Points than shown on the table for your level. You regain all spent Transmog Points when you finish a long rest.

Reassimilate

You can destroy one of your Constructs, reabsorbing their potential energy. You regain the Constructs Spell level plus 1 Transmog Points.

Transmogrification Options - lvl. 2

When you gain access to the Transmogrification Initiate Feature, you gain access to the following Initiate options for spending your Transmog Points.

Empowering Reserves

When you make an ability check on your turn, you can spend 2 sorcery points to gain advantage on the check.

Imbuing Touch

As an action, you can touch one non-magical weapon and spend 2 sorcery points to imbue it with magic for 1 minute. For the duration, the weapon is considered magical for the purpose of overcoming immunity and resistance to non-magical attacks.

Sorcerous Fortitude

As an action, you can spend any number of sorcery points to roll a d4 for each point expended. You gain a number of temporary hit points equal to the total rolled, or your Constitution Modifier (per die), whichever is higher.
